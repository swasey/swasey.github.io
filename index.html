I am a Ph.D. student advised by Deepak Garg. Prior to joining the MPI-SWS in July 2012, I was a research programmer at Carnegie Mellon University working under the direction of Lujo Bauer (2006-12), Bob Harper (1998-2006), and Roger Dannenberg (1994-98).

Research Interests

I am interested in applying techniques from programming language theory, proof systems, and formal verification to problems in computer security.

In my initial research, I will focus on techniques for compositional reasoning about secure systems. One of my concrete goals is to develop a higher-order version of Garg et al's framework for modeling and proving security properties in the presence of interface-confined adversaries.
